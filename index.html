Committee on Student Fees

UC Berkeley

About

The Committee on Student Fees and Budget Review (CSF) is a Chancellor's Advisory Committee reporting to the Chancellor and his designated representatives. CSF works closely with campus executive leadership, management, and staff to recommend on the management and allocation of a $33 million Student Services Fee (SSF) Fund.

Each academic year, CSF evaluates select campus units that receive funding allocations from the SSF and reports on their current activities and financial health. Past evaluations include ones of Recreational Sports, University Health Services (UHS), Cal Performances, the Career Center, and University of California Police Department (UCPD).

In conjunction with the Associated Students of the University of California (ASUC) and the Graduate Assembly (GA), CSF also conducts an annual, campus-wide survey that gauges student sentiment regarding SSF funded campus units. Campus unit analyses, survey findings, and suggested funding strategies are reported in a Budget Recommendation to the Chancellor at the end of each academic year.

Members of CSF serve as representatives on external student fee advisory committees on campus including, but not limited to, the Chancellor's Advisory Committee on Student Services and Fees (CACSSF), Student Fee Referenda Committee, and the Health Fee Advisory Board. CSF's participation in these Committees provides for a holistic view of how student fees are implemented on campus.

On a state-wide level, CSF represents UC Berkeley as a member of the Council on Student Fees — a UC system-wide organization composed of representatives from "CSF-analogous bodies" at each UC campus. As part of the Council, CSF plays an integral role in the oversight of policies governing student fees on a UC system-wide level and assists with providing recommendations to the UC Office of the President.
